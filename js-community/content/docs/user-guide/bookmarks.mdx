# Bookmarks

Bookmarks let you save any post for later reading. They are personal — other users cannot see your bookmarks.

## Adding a bookmark

Click the **bookmark icon** on any post. The icon fills to confirm the save. You can bookmark:

- The opening post of a topic (effectively bookmarking the whole thread)
- Any individual reply within a topic

## Viewing your bookmarks

Navigate to:

```
/forum/u/[username]/bookmarks
```

Your bookmarks are listed in reverse-chronological order (most recently bookmarked first). Each entry shows:

- The post content excerpt
- The topic title and category
- A link back to the exact post in context

## Removing a bookmark

Click the bookmark icon again on any bookmarked post to remove it. The icon returns to its unfilled state and the post disappears from your bookmarks list.

## Use cases

- **Reference material** — bookmark posts with code solutions, configuration snippets, or links you want to find again
- **Reading list** — bookmark long threads you want to finish reading later
- **Curated answers** — bookmark the most helpful reply in a thread rather than the whole topic

## Bookmarks and search

Bookmarks are not currently included in the platform-wide search. To find a specific saved post, visit your bookmarks page directly at `/forum/u/[username]/bookmarks`.
