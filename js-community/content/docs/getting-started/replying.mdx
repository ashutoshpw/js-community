# Replying to Posts

Replies are posts made within an existing topic. Each reply increments the topic's post count and bumps it in the Latest feed.

## Posting a reply

At the bottom of every open topic there is a **Reply** button. Clicking it opens the composer pre-filled with the topic context.

- Write your reply in Markdown
- Click **Post Reply** to submit

Your reply gets the next `postNumber` in the thread. If someone is reading the topic in real time, they see a "New reply" prompt without needing to refresh.

## Replying to a specific post

To reply directly to a post rather than the topic generally:

1. Click the **Reply** icon on the specific post (not the bottom reply button)
2. Your reply is linked to that post via `replyToPostNumber`
3. Readers see a "In reply to @username" indicator at the top of your post

This creates a visible threading chain — useful in long discussions where multiple sub-conversations are happening in parallel.

## Quoting a post

To include part of another user's post in your reply:

1. Select the text you want to quote
2. A **Quote** button appears — click it
3. The selected text is inserted into the composer in blockquote Markdown:

```markdown
> This is the quoted text
```

You can quote from multiple posts in a single reply.

## Liking a post

Click the **heart** (❤) icon at the bottom of any post to like it. Likes contribute to:

- The post's `likeCount`
- The topic's overall `likeCount`
- The author's progress toward higher trust levels
- The topic appearing in the **Top** feed

You can unlike a post by clicking the heart again.

## Editing your reply

Click the **Edit** (pencil) icon on your own post to edit it. Each edit creates a new version. The post displays a "last edited" timestamp for transparency.

You cannot edit other users' posts. Moderators can edit any post.

## Deleting a reply

You can delete your own post using the **Delete** action in the post menu. Deleted posts are soft-deleted — they are hidden from members but remain in the database and can be restored by admins.

The topic's `postsCount` is updated when a post is deleted.
